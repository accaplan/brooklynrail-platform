--- title: WIDE STEPS UP (FOR JOHN GIORNO) ---

Shifting scale of persons to surroundings The white rocks shrink and grow Gravely in the night This earth cannot live without holy rites. Lots of hills to climb up and down. A straight ravine between the friars. Turn to the right And you will fall to the left. One pilgrim wears a beard Down to his chest But love hates coverings. And prefers to be seen and, if despised, To melt away quickly. No gloss is allowed To weaken the bliss of scripture.
