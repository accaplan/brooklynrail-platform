--- title: 'Dixie Don’t Shoot' ---

Every Fifth Word for Five Repetitions —for John Sims’s Afro-Dixie Remix Listening Party at the Bowery Poetry Club
November 2, 2015

 

I.

Oh, I wish I was in the land of cotton, Cinnamon seed and sandy bottom, Look away, look away, look away Dixie Land. In Dixie Land, where I was born in, early on one frosty mornin’, Look away, look away, look away Dixie Land. I wish I was in Dixie, Hooray! Hooray! In Dixie Land I’ll take my stand to live and die in Dixie. Ole Missus marry “Will the weaver” Willum was a gay deceiver But when he put his arm around ’er, He smiled fierce as a forty pounder, His face was sharp as a butcher’s cleaver But that did not seem to grieve ’er Ole Missus acted the foolish part And died for a man that broke her heart Now here’s a health to the next ole Missus An’ all the gals that want to kiss us; But if you want to drive ’way sorrow Come and hear this song tomorrow There’s buckwheat cakes and Injun batter, Makes you fat or a little fatter Then hoe it down and scratch your gravel, To Dixie’s Land I’m bound to travel, Look away! Look away! Look away! Dixie Land
